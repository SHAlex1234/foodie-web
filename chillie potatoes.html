Chilli Potatoes

Meals in Sri Lanka typically include rice, a variety of vegetables, sambals, and meat dishes. One of the most well-known recipes made with boiling potatoes and spices like cinnamon, garlic, and fiery red chilli flakes is Sri Lankan chilli potatoes. The dish is served with gently spiced Sri Lankan dhal and rice, and it is moderately hot. This dish typically calls for dry Maldivian fish, but we've left it out to make it entirely vegetarian. For a delectable dinner with Sri Lankan influences for the weekend lunch, serve the Sri Lankan Chilli Potatoes Recipe with Kerala Matta Rice, Sri Lankan Dal, Sambol, and Sri Lankan Chicken Curry.

prep time

10 min.

cook time

10 min.

serving

4 servings

Tags : beef breakfast pancakes food

instructions

step 1

Wash and pressure cook the potatoes for 2 whistles. Peel the skin and cut into big cubes and keep aside.

step 2

Roughly pound the garlic cloves using a pestle and mortar and keep aside.

step 3

Heat oil in a heavy bottomed pan, add cinnamon stick, mustard seeds, curry leaves and let it crackle.

step 4

Add the pounded garlic and let its flavour infuse into the oil. Add the sliced onions and saute until it turns translucent.

step 5

Add the turmeric powder, red chilli powder, red chili flakes and mix well. Now, add the boiled and cubed potatoes, season with salt and mix well.

step 6

Let the potato mix slow cook with the spices for 10 minutes, occasionally keep stirring so that it does not stick to the bottom of the pan.

step 7

Once the potates are fully coated with the spices and has come together, add in the lemon juice, mix well and turn off the heat.

step 8

Serve the Sri Lankan Chili Potatoes Recipe along with Kerala Matta Rice, Sri Lankan Dal, Sambol and Sri Lankan Chicken Curry for a delicious Sri Lankan inspired menu for the weekend lunch.

ingredients

500 g Potatoes boiled

1 inch Cinnamon Stick

8 cloves garlic

1 Onion thinly sliced

1/2 tsp Turmeric powder

1/2 tsp Red Chillie powder

2 tbsp Red Chillie flakes

1 tsp Mustard seeds

1 sprig Curry leaves

2 tsp lemon juice

1 tbsp Oil

Salt to taste

tools

Pressure Cooker

Large Heavy Pot With Lid

Measuring Spoons

Measuring Cups
